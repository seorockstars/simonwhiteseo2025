How to Find and Hire the Best Local SEO Company

Introduction

Finding the best local SEO company for your business is a critical investment in your online visibility and growth. Local search engine optimization helps your business appear prominently in local search results, Google Maps, and local directoriesâ€”essential for attracting customers in your geographic area. This guide will walk you through a systematic process to evaluate, compare, and select a local SEO company that delivers measurable results for your specific business needs.

By following these steps, you'll learn how to identify qualified SEO providers, assess their track record, understand their strategies, and make an informed decision that aligns with your budget and business objectives.

Materials/Prerequisites

  • Your business website URL
  • Current Google Analytics account (or access to it)
  • Google My Business account
  • Your primary business keywords and service area
  • Budget allocated for SEO services
  • List of 3-5 competitor websites
  • Spreadsheet or document for tracking company information
  • Access to review platforms (Google, Yelp, Trustpilot)

Step 1: Define Your Local SEO Goals and Budget

Before searching for a local SEO company, clearly establish what you want to achieve and how much you're willing to invest.

  1. Identify your primary geographic service area (city, county, or multi-location)
  2. Determine your main business keywords (e.g., "plumber in Denver" or "Italian restaurant near me")
  3. Set specific goals: rank in top 3 Google Maps results, increase local website traffic by 50%, or generate 20+ qualified leads monthly
  4. Establish your monthly budget range ($500-$5,000+ depending on business size and competition)
  5. Document your current baseline metrics: website traffic, phone calls, online inquiries, and local ranking positions

Step 2: Research and Compile a List of Candidate SEO Companies

Identify potential local SEO providers through multiple channels to build a comprehensive list.

  1. Search "local SEO company near [your city]" and "SEO services [your industry]" on Google
  2. Check online review platforms: Google Business Profile, Yelp, Trustpilot, and Better Business Bureau
  3. Review websites of your top 5 local competitors to see which SEO agencies they use (check "powered by" or footer mentions)
  4. Ask for referrals from business colleagues, local chamber of commerce, or industry associations
  5. Verify each company has a professional website with clear service descriptions and case studies
  6. Compile 8-12 qualified candidates into a spreadsheet with contact information and initial observations

Step 3: Evaluate Company Credentials and Track Record

Assess the legitimacy and expertise of each candidate company through their certifications and portfolio.

  1. Verify Google Partner status or certification on Google's official partner directory
  2. Check for industry certifications: HubSpot Academy, Moz, SEMrush Academy, or similar
  3. Review their case studies and client portfolioâ€”prioritize those with results in your industry or similar service areas
  4. Look for specific metrics: "increased local traffic by 150%" or "improved rankings from position 8 to position 2"
  5. Confirm they have at least 5+ years of local SEO experience with proven client retention
  6. Request references from 3-5 past or current clients in your geographic area

Step 4: Assess Their Local SEO Strategy and Approach

Request detailed information about how each company approaches local search optimization.

  1. Ask each company to explain their local SEO methodology in writing
  2. Verify they address key local SEO components:
    • Google My Business optimization and management
    • Local citation building and consistency
    • Local keyword research and on-page optimization
    • Local link building and authority development
    • Review management and local reputation monitoring
  3. Confirm they provide monthly reporting with specific metrics (rankings, traffic, conversions, calls)
  4. Ask about their tools: SEMrush, Ahrefs, Moz, Google Search Console, or proprietary platforms
  5. Request a detailed proposal outlining timeline, deliverables, and expected outcomes (avoid guarantees of rankings)

Step 5: Conduct Discovery Calls and Compare Proposals

Schedule consultations with your top 3 candidates to evaluate communication and compatibility.

  1. Prepare a list of 10-15 specific questions about their approach, timeline, and pricing
  2. Schedule 20-30 minute discovery calls with each company's team
  3. Evaluate their communication style: Are they responsive, clear, and focused on your goals?
  4. Ask about their typical client onboarding timeline (usually 4-8 weeks before initial results appear)
  5. Request written proposals from each with pricing, scope, duration, and expected deliverables
  6. Negotiate terms: many companies offer package discounts or trial periods (3-6 months)

Step 6: Review Client References and Make Your Final Decision

Validate each company's claims by speaking directly with their clients.

  1. Contact all provided references; ask specific questions:
    • What results did you see, and in what timeframe?
    • How is their communication and responsiveness?
    • Would you hire them again?
    • What are their strengths and weaknesses?
  2. Check online reviews again, focusing on recent feedback (last 6 months)
  3. Compare proposals side-by-side: pricing, timeline, services, and communication approach
  4. Trust your instincts: choose the company where you feel confident in their expertise and communication
  5. Confirm there are no contracts longer than 12 months or cancellation penalties
  6. Request a 30-day service agreement for your first month to ensure fit

Conclusion

Selecting the best local SEO company requires thorough research, clear communication, and careful evaluation of credentials and track record. By following these six steps, you've moved from initial research through detailed comparison to making an informed decision aligned with your business goals.

After hiring your local SEO company, establish monthly check-in meetings, set clear performance benchmarks, and give the strategy 3-6 months to produce significant results. Remember that local SEO is an ongoing investmentâ€”avoid companies promising immediate rankings or guaranteeing top positions, as legitimate SEO requires time and consistent effort to deliver sustainable results.
